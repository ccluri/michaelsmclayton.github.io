MICHAEL S. CLAYTON (PhD)

neuroscientist & programmer

ABOUT ME

I was born in southern England in November 1990. After receiving a BSc in Psychology from the University of Exeter in 2014, I completed a PhD in Experimental Psychology at the University of Oxford (2014 - 2018). Here, I focused on how visual attention might be supported by oscillations in human brain activity. I sought to determine the role of a specific neural oscillation (known as 'occipitopareital alpha') by delivering electrical currents that oscillated in synchrony with this oscillation, and observing the consequences for visual attention task performance. More recently, after having submitted by PhD thesis, I worked as a Science Intern for Cambridge Cognition. Here, I learned about software development life cycles, and how to run cognitive psychology experiment online.

INTERESTS

THE NEUROSCIENCE OF VISUAL PERCEPTION

I am profoundly interested in the neural processes that support visual perception.

NEURAL OSCILLATIONS AND NEUROIMAGING

I am also very much interested in rhythmic brain activity, and the roles such rhythms play in cognitive processes

PROGRAMMING LANGUAGES

During my experiences with academic research, I discovered a passion for programming languages. Some of the most enjoyable experiences from my PhD were had when programming cognitive tasks and analysis scripts. I learned MATLAB, R, and Python during these doctoral years.
More recently, I have become interested in programming languages uses on the web, and have learned HTML, CSS, and Javascript
